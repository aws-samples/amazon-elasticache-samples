# StrideMax Cushion Pro Community Q&A (Sample)

## Q1: Is this good for flat feet?
**Answer (Brand Rep):** Many customers with flat feet appreciate the extra cushioning. However, StrideMax Cushion Pro does not have built-in arch support. Customers with flat feet may benefit from adding a custom insole.

## Q2: How does this compare to AeroFlex Runner 2?
**Answer (Brand Rep):** StrideMax Cushion Pro has significantly more cushioning and a rocker geometry for smoother transitions. AeroFlex Runner 2 is lighter and more responsive. StrideMax is better for long runs and recovery; AeroFlex is better for daily shorter runs.

## Q3: Can I use these for walking?
**Answer (Community):** Absolutely. I use mine for 5-mile walks and they're incredibly comfortable. The rocker shape actually makes walking feel easier.

## Q4: How many miles do these last?
**Answer (Community):** I got about 400 miles before the cushioning felt noticeably flatter. Outsole was still fine at that point.

## Q5: Are these heavy?
**Answer (Brand Rep):** At 10.8 oz (men's size 10), they are heavier than AeroFlex Runner 2 (8.9 oz) due to the additional cushioning material. Most customers find the comfort trade-off worthwhile for longer distances.
