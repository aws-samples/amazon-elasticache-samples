# Running Shoes Fit & Sizing Guide (Accent Athletics)

## How to Choose the Right Size
For running shoes, customers often prefer about a thumb-width of space in front of the longest toe to account for foot swelling during activity. Fit preference varies by use case (running vs casual wear) and sock thickness.

## General Fit Guidance by Model

### AeroFlex Runner 2
- Length: Generally true to size
- Width: Standard
- Toe box feel: Moderate to slightly snug for some customers
- Best for: Customers who like a secure everyday fit

### AeroFlex Runner 2 Wide
- Length: Generally true to size
- Width: Wide forefoot / roomier toe box
- Best for: Wide feet, bunions, customers who dislike toe pressure

### AeroFlex Walker
- Length: True to size
- Width: Slightly roomier than AeroFlex Runner 2
- Best for: All-day walking and standing; customers wanting casual styling

### StrideMax Cushion Pro
- Length: Generally true to size
- Width: Slightly roomier than AeroFlex Runner 2
- Feel: Softer and more cushioned, more volume overall

### TrailCore Grip 5 / TrailCore Grip 5 GTX
- Length: True to size for most customers
- Width: Standard
- Feel: More structured upper and firmer platform than road models
- Note: GTX version may feel slightly warmer due to membrane

### SprintEdge Racer
- Length: True to size
- Width: Narrow race fit; no wide option
- Feel: Snug and locked-down; not for wide feet
- Best for: Race day and speed workouts only

### TempoRun Interval Trainer
- Length: True to size
- Width: Snugger than AeroFlex Runner 2; narrower heel cup
- Best for: Speed workouts and tempo runs

### FlexForm Training Shoe
- Length: True to size
- Width: Standard; midfoot strap adjusts snugness
- Feel: Firmer than running shoes; flat stable base

### CloudWalk Recovery Slide
- Length: True to size; unisex sizing
- Width: Relaxed open-toe fit
- Not intended for running or high-impact activity

## Fit Tips for Support Agents
- If customer reports toe pressure, suggest checking sock thickness and trying wide version when available
- If customer wants soft cushioning for long wear, compare with StrideMax Cushion Pro
- If customer plans trail use, recommend trail-specific model instead of road model
- If customer wants a race shoe, confirm they understand limited durability and narrow fit
- For gym/lifting, always recommend FlexForm over running shoes

## Common Customer Phrases and How to Interpret
- "Feels tight in front" -> possible forefoot width issue
- "Heel slipping" -> may need lace adjustment, different sock, or different size
- "Too soft" -> customer may prefer a firmer daily trainer
- "Too firm" -> suggest StrideMax Cushion Pro or CloudWalk Recovery Slide
- "Need something for the gym" -> FlexForm Training Shoe
- "Need something for trails" -> TrailCore Grip 5 (or GTX for wet conditions)
