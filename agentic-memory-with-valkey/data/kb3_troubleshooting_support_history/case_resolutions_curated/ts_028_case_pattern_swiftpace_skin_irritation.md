# Case Pattern: SwiftPace Watch Band Causes Skin Irritation

## Pattern Summary
Low-to-moderate frequency (avg 4 cases/week). Customer reports redness, itching, or rash under the watch band after wearing SwiftPace for extended periods.

## Root Cause
Multiple possible causes:
1. **Moisture trapped under band:** Most common. Sweat during exercise stays trapped, causing contact dermatitis. Not an allergic reaction — it's irritation from prolonged moisture contact.
2. **Band too tight:** Customers tighten band for better HR readings, restricting airflow.
3. **Nickel sensitivity:** SwiftPace buckle contains trace nickel. ~10% of population has nickel sensitivity. Causes localized rash at buckle contact point specifically.
4. **Soap/sunscreen residue:** Products trapped between band and skin cause irritation.

## Resolution
1. Ask where the irritation is: under the band (moisture/tightness) or at the buckle (nickel).
2. **Under band:**
   - Loosen band one notch. HR accuracy loss is minimal at one notch.
   - Dry wrist and band after exercise. Remove watch for 1 hour after workouts.
   - Switch wrists periodically.
   - Wash band weekly with mild soap and water, dry completely before wearing.
3. **At buckle (nickel):**
   - Offer free replacement band with plastic buckle (no nickel). Order via support portal → Accessories → "SwiftPace Hypoallergenic Band." Ships free.
   - Alternatively: customer can apply clear nail polish to buckle interior as barrier (common dermatologist recommendation).
4. **If rash is severe (blistering, spreading beyond watch area):** Recommend customer see a dermatologist. This is beyond product support scope. Do NOT diagnose.

## Stats
- First-contact resolution rate: 91%
- Avg handle time: 7 min
- Free band replacement rate: 22% of cases (nickel sensitivity)
