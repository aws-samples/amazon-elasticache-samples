# Case Pattern: SleepSync Tracks Pet Instead of Human

## Pattern Summary
Low frequency but memorable (avg 2 cases/week). Customer reports SleepSync sleep data shows unusual patterns — very high breathing rate, excessive movement, or "No Sleeper Detected" alternating with tracking. Investigation reveals a pet (usually dog or cat) sleeps on the bed.

## Root Cause
SleepSync radar cannot distinguish between human and animal breathing/movement. If a large pet (>20 lbs) sleeps closer to the device than the human, it will track the pet.

## Resolution
1. Ask: "Does a pet sleep on the bed?" (This resolves the mystery in ~90% of these cases.)
2. Reposition SleepSync to aim directly at the human, away from pet's usual sleeping spot.
3. Narrow the tracking zone: App → Devices → SleepSync → Tracking Zone → set to "Narrow." This reduces the radar cone from 120° to 60°, making it more directional.
4. If pet sleeps between device and human: move device to other side of bed, or mount on wall behind headboard aimed down at human.
5. If pet moves around the bed unpredictably: there's no reliable fix. SleepSync will intermittently track the pet. Recommend placing device on a shelf above the bed aimed downward (pet is less likely to be at head height).

## Tone Guidance
This is a lighthearted issue — customers usually find it funny once they realize what's happening. Match their energy. It's okay to say something like "Mystery solved — your dog has been getting their sleep tracked!" Keep it professional but warm.

## Stats
- First-contact resolution rate: 95%
- Avg handle time: 5 min
- Customer satisfaction: 4.8/5 (highest of any case pattern — customers love the pet revelation)
