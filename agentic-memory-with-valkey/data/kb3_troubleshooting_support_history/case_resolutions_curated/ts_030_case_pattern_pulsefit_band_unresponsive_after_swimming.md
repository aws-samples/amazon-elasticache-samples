# Case Pattern: PulseFit Band 4 Display Unresponsive After Swimming

## Pattern Summary
Moderate frequency (avg 5 cases/week, spikes in summer). Customer reports PulseFit Band 4 touchscreen is unresponsive or erratic after swimming. Device appears powered on (LED active) but touch input doesn't register.

## Root Cause
PulseFit Band 4 is water-resistant to 50m (5 ATM) — the hardware is fine. The issue is **water on the touchscreen surface**. Capacitive touch screens cannot distinguish between finger touch and water droplets. Water on the screen registers as phantom touches or blocks real touches.

## Resolution
1. **Reassure:** "Your PulseFit Band is designed for swimming and the water hasn't damaged it. The touchscreen just needs to be dried off."
2. Dry the screen and wrist area thoroughly with a towel.
3. If screen is still erratic: enable Water Lock mode BEFORE swimming next time. Swipe up on watch face → tap water drop icon. This disables touch input during swimming and uses button-only navigation. To exit Water Lock: press and hold side button for 3 seconds — watch will vibrate and expel water from speaker port.
4. If touchscreen remains unresponsive after thorough drying (wait 10+ minutes): possible water ingress past seals. Check for visible moisture under screen. If yes → warranty replacement (seal failure is a manufacturing defect).

## Prevention Advice
"For future swims, we recommend activating Water Lock mode before getting in the water. This prevents accidental touches and helps expel water from the speaker when you're done."

## Stats
- First-contact resolution rate: 94%
- Avg handle time: 5 min
- Actual water ingress (hardware defect): <3% of cases
