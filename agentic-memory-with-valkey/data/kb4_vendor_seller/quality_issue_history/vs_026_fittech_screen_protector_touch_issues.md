# FitTech Solutions: Third-Party Screen Protector Causing SwiftPace Touch Issues

## Summary
FitTech bundles a third-party tempered glass screen protector with SwiftPace Running Watch orders. Multiple customers report touchscreen responsiveness issues after applying the bundled protector.

## Timeline
- Jan 15 – Feb 20, 2026: 31 complaints about SwiftPace touch responsiveness from FitTech customers
- Feb 18: Pattern identified — all affected customers had applied the FitTech-bundled screen protector
- Feb 20: FitTech notified. They are switching screen protector supplier.

## Root Cause
The third-party screen protector (brand: "ShieldMax") is 0.5mm thick — too thick for the SwiftPace's capacitive touch sensor to register reliably through. The official Accent Athletics screen protector (sold separately, $12.99) is 0.2mm and designed for the SwiftPace touch sensitivity.

## Agent Action
If customer reports SwiftPace touch issues and purchased from FitTech:
1. Ask: "Did you apply a screen protector to your watch?"
2. If yes: "The screen protector bundled with your FitTech order may be interfering with the touchscreen. Try removing it and testing the touch responsiveness."
3. If touch works fine without protector: issue confirmed. Recommend the official Accent Athletics screen protector as a replacement.
4. If touch is still unresponsive without protector: proceed with standard SwiftPace troubleshooting. May be a separate issue.

## CRITICAL: Do Not Confuse With
- SwiftPace firmware bricking (ts_022) — completely different issue (black screen, not touch)
- PulseFit Band 4 unresponsive after swimming (ts_030) — water on screen, not protector
- This is a **third-party accessory issue**, not an Accent Athletics product defect

## FitTech Responsibility
FitTech has agreed to:
- Refund the screen protector cost ($4.99) to any customer who reports this issue
- Switch to a thinner protector for future orders
- Include a disclaimer that the bundled protector is not an official Accent Athletics accessory
