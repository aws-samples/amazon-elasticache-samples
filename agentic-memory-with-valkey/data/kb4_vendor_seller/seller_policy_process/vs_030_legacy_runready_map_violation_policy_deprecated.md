# Legacy RunReady MAP Violation Policy (Deprecated)
**Status:** Deprecated

> **Status: DEPRECATED** | Last updated: 2025-12-01
> **Superseded by:** Updated Marketplace Seller Agreement (2026 edition) — MAP enforcement now handled by Brand Protection team, not Marketplace Support.

## Original Content (Do Not Use)
This document described the 2025 MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) violation process for RunReady:
- First violation: email warning from Marketplace Support
- Second violation: 7-day listing suspension
- Third violation: seller account suspension

## Why Deprecated
As of Jan 1, 2026, MAP enforcement for all sellers was transferred from Marketplace Support to the Brand Protection team. The escalation path and penalties have changed. Marketplace Support agents should NOT issue MAP warnings or suspensions.

**If an agent notices a seller advertising below MAP:** File a ticket to Brand Protection (category: MAP-VIOLATION) with the listing URL and screenshot. Do not contact the seller directly.

## Common Mistake
Agents sometimes reference this deprecated policy when a customer asks "why is this product cheaper on [seller] than on your website?" The answer is NOT "they're violating MAP" — it may be a legitimate promotion, open-box pricing, or the seller may have negotiated different terms. Simply say: "Pricing may vary by seller. Our authorized sellers set their own prices."
