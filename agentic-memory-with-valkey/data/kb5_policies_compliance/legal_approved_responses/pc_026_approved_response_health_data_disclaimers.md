# Approved Response Templates: Health Data and Wellness Device Disclaimers
**Template Version:** LEG-HEALTH-DISC-US-v1.2
**Status:** Approved (Internal Use Only)
**Effective:** 2026-01-01

## Purpose
Legal-approved disclaimers agents must use when discussing health-related data from Accent Athletics connected devices. These disclaimers protect both the customer and the company.

## Template 1: General Health Data Disclaimer (Use in Every Health-Related Interaction)
> "Please note that Accent Athletics wellness devices are designed for fitness and wellness tracking purposes and are not medical devices. The data provided — including heart rate, blood oxygen, sleep stages, and body composition — is intended for general wellness information only and should not be used to diagnose, treat, or prevent any medical condition."

## Template 2: SleepSync Apnea Detection Disclaimer
> "The SleepSync apnea detection feature is a wellness screening tool, not a medical diagnostic. It may indicate patterns that are worth discussing with your healthcare provider, but it cannot diagnose sleep apnea. Only a qualified healthcare professional can diagnose sleep disorders."

**CRITICAL:** During the current false apnea alert issue (KB3: ts_021), lead with the known issue explanation FIRST, then provide this disclaimer if the customer asks about the medical validity of the feature.

## Template 3: StrideScale Body Composition Disclaimer
> "StrideScale body composition measurements (body fat percentage, muscle mass, bone mass, water percentage) use bioelectrical impedance analysis, which provides estimates rather than exact measurements. Results can vary based on hydration, time of day, and other factors. For clinical-grade body composition analysis, please consult your healthcare provider."

**Note:** During the current body fat spike issue (KB3: ts_020), explain the software calibration issue FIRST. Then use this disclaimer if customer asks about accuracy in general.

## Template 4: Heart Rate / Blood Oxygen Disclaimer
> "The optical heart rate and blood oxygen sensors in SwiftPace and PulseFit Band provide estimates for fitness tracking purposes. Accuracy may vary based on fit, skin type, activity level, and environmental conditions. These readings should not be used for medical decisions. If you have concerns about your heart rate or blood oxygen levels, please consult your healthcare provider."

## Template 5: Customer Asks "Should I See a Doctor?"
> "I'm not able to provide medical advice, but if you have any health concerns, it's always a good idea to consult with your healthcare provider. They can provide personalized guidance based on your individual health situation."

**Do NOT say:** "You should see a doctor" (implies urgency/diagnosis) or "You don't need to see a doctor" (dismissive of concern). The template above is the approved neutral response.

## When to Use These Disclaimers
- Customer asks about the medical accuracy of any device reading
- Customer makes a health decision based on device data
- Customer reports a health concern triggered by device data (e.g., apnea alert, abnormal HR)
- Customer asks if device data can be shared with their doctor (answer: yes, via app export, but include Template 1 disclaimer)
