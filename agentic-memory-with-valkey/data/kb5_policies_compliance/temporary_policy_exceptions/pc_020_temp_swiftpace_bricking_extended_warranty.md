# Temporary Policy Exception: SwiftPace Firmware Bricking — Extended Warranty Replacement
**Exception ID:** TEMP-WAR-SWIFTPACE-BRICK-2026-02
**Region:** US
**Status:** Active
**Effective:** 2026-02-22 to 2026-04-30
**Overrides:** WAR-ELEC-US-v2.0 (pc_011)
**Exception Priority:** 1 (highest)

## Exception
Due to the SwiftPace firmware v3.2.0 bricking issue (KB3: ts_022), the following temporary warranty exception is in effect:

**Any SwiftPace Running Watch bricked during the v3.2.0 update is eligible for free replacement regardless of:**
- Purchase date (even if outside 1-year warranty)
- Purchase source (including GearVault renewed units — overrides 90-day GearVault warranty)
- Physical condition of the watch prior to bricking

## Rationale
This is a manufacturer-caused defect. The firmware update was pushed by Accent Athletics to all watches. Customers did not opt into a risky action — the update was presented as routine. Accent Athletics bears full responsibility.

## Process
1. Customer reports bricked SwiftPace → follow KB3: ts_022 recovery steps
2. If recovery fails → initiate warranty replacement with exception code BRICK-2026
3. **Expedited shipping:** replacement ships next business day (not standard 3–5 day)
4. **No return required:** customer does NOT need to ship back the bricked watch (it's non-functional anyway). Mark as "manufacturer defect write-off."

## Scope Limits
- This exception applies ONLY to watches bricked during the v3.2.0 update
- Does NOT apply to watches with other issues (cracked screen, water damage, etc.) even if they're also out of warranty
- Does NOT apply to counterfeit watches from PeakForm (KB4: vs_015) — those were never genuine and never received the real firmware update

## Expiration
Exception expires Apr 30, 2026. After that date, any remaining bricking claims revert to standard warranty policy (pc_011). The expectation is that all affected customers will have contacted support by then.
