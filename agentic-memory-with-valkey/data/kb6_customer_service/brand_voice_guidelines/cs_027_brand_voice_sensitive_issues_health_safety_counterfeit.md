# Brand Voice Guidelines: Handling Sensitive Issues (Health, Safety, Counterfeit)

## Purpose
Supplement to the main brand voice guidelines (cs_005) for emotionally sensitive customer interactions.

## Principle: Match the Gravity of the Situation
Our standard brand voice is warm, helpful, and slightly casual. But some situations require a more serious, empathetic tone. Don't be chipper when a customer is scared.

## Health-Related Interactions (SleepSync, StrideScale, HR Alerts)

### DO
- "I understand this alert is concerning."
- "Let me explain what's happening with your device."
- "This is a known software issue, not a reflection of your health."
- "If you have any health concerns, we always recommend consulting your healthcare provider."

### DON'T
- "Don't worry about it!" (dismissive)
- "It's just a glitch, no big deal." (minimizes their concern)
- "You should probably see a doctor." (creates panic, we're not qualified)
- "Our device is very accurate." (overpromises, especially during known issues)

- SleepSync apnea handling: KB3 ts_021

## Safety Incidents (Overheating, Fire Risk, Injury)

### DO
- "I'm glad you're safe. Thank you for reporting this."
- "Please discontinue use of the device."
- "I'm documenting this for our safety team to investigate."
- "I'd like to process a full refund and [offer credit]."

### DON'T
- "That's unusual, I've never heard of that happening." (minimizes, implies customer is exaggerating)
- "Are you sure it was our product?" (accusatory)
- "It's probably fine to keep using it." (liability risk)
- "That sounds like it could be dangerous!" (amplifies fear unnecessarily)

### Reference
- Safety intake templates: KB5 pc_010
- Safety follow-up templates: KB5 pc_025
- PeakForm counterfeit safety: KB4 vs_029

## Counterfeit Product Conversations

### DO
- "This seller has been removed from our marketplace."
- "Some products from this seller did not meet our authenticity standards."
- "I can process a full refund for you right now."
- "I'd be happy to help you find the genuine version."

### DON'T
- "You bought a fake." (blunt, makes customer feel foolish)
- "That seller was selling counterfeits." (legal language to avoid)
- "You should have checked the reviews." (victim-blaming)
- "We're suing them." (never reference legal proceedings)
