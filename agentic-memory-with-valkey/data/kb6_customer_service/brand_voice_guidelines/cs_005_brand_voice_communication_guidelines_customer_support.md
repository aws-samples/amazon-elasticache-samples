# Brand Voice and Communication Guidelines (Customer Support)

## Purpose
Define how support agents communicate with customers across chat, email, and phone follow-up messaging.

## Voice Principles
1. **Clear**  
Use plain language and direct next steps.

2. **Calm**  
Stay composed, especially in escalations or delays.

3. **Respectful**  
Acknowledge inconvenience without assigning blame.

4. **Accurate**  
Do not guess, over-promise, or invent timelines.

5. **Action-oriented**  
Tell the customer what you can do next.

## Recommended Language Patterns
- "I can help check that for you."
- "Here is what I can confirm right now."
- "I can help review the next steps."
- "Based on the order details available..."
- "If you would like, I can escalate this for review."

## Avoid
- "Definitely" or "guaranteed" unless policy/commitment explicitly supports it
- Blaming statements about carriers, sellers, or other teams without evidence
- Legal conclusions in support conversations
- Dismissive phrasing ("You just need to...", "That's not our problem")

## De-escalation Guidance
- Acknowledge impact first
- Share one clear next step
- Avoid repeating policy language without context
- If denying a request, pair with what can still be done (review, escalation, alternatives)

## Channel Notes

### Chat
- Short paragraphs
- One action step at a time for troubleshooting
- Confirm before moving to higher-effort steps (reinstall/re-pair/etc.) when applicable

### Email
- Summarize issue + action taken
- Include timeline expectations within approved ranges
- Use macros as base, then personalize

## Policy-Sensitive Topics
For privacy, safety, denied returns, or legal escalations, use approved legal/compliance templates and avoid freeform phrasing that changes meaning.
