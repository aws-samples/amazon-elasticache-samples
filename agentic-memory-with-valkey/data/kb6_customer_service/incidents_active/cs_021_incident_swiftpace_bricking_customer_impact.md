# Incident Bulletin: SwiftPace Firmware v3.2.0 Bricking — Customer Impact

**Incident ID:** INC-PROD-SWIFTPACE-BRICK-2026-02
**Status:** Active (firmware pulled, replacements in progress)
**Severity:** Critical
**Opened:** 2026-02-22
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-26

## Summary
SwiftPace Running Watch firmware v3.2.0 caused some watches (Rev A/B hardware) to brick during update. Firmware has been pulled from update servers. Estimated 2,000–3,000 watches affected globally.

## Customer Impact
- Affected customers have a non-functional watch
- Marathon/race season is active — many customers need their watch urgently
- Social media mentions increasing — #SwiftPaceBrick trending on running forums

## Agent Actions
1. **Identify:** Customer reports black screen / unresponsive SwiftPace after update
2. **Recover:** Follow KB3: ts_022 recovery steps (hard reset → USB recovery)
3. **Replace:** If recovery fails, initiate expedited warranty replacement per KB5: pc_020 exception (no warranty check, no return required, next-day shipping)
4. **Credit:** Offer $25 goodwill credit per KB3: ts_022 guidelines
5. **Macro:** Use ELEC-KI-001 (cs_016) for initial response, WAR-005 (cs_019) for replacement

## What NOT to Do
- Do NOT tell customer to "wait for the next update" — their watch is bricked, there's nothing to update
- Do NOT ask for proof of purchase or warranty verification — pc_020 exception waives this
- Do NOT redirect GearVault customers to GearVault — pc_020 overrides GearVault's 90-day warranty for this specific issue
- Do NOT blame the customer for updating — the update was pushed by Accent Athletics
